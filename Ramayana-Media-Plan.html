Ramayana · Companion media plan

Ramayana: Media Plan

₹250 crore, built to turn a domestic epic into a global event. Companion to the case study.

Currency in ₹ crore unless stated. Film: Ramayana Part One, dir. Nitesh Tiwari, prod. Namit Malhotra / DNEG. Production cost ~₹835cr; release Diwali (8 Nov 2026) (Outlook Business). All allocations reconcile to ₹250cr.

01

Positioning: one film, two doors

The film is one thing; the way it's sold runs on two tracks, plus a bridge.

TrackPromiseTone & assets
India"Witness this grand event, the Ramayana as you have never seen it."Pride, reverence, nostalgia (the 1987 serial), spectacle, devotional music. Family, festival, big-screen.
World (crossover)A mythic epic, India's serious swing at a world-class film, a 5,000-year-old story told with Hollywood craft.Scale, VFX, battle set-pieces, the Zimmer score, IMAX as event. No song breaks, no melodrama in the cut.
Diaspora (bridge)"Your roots, made world-class. Show your children who they are."A deliberate hybrid: pride and prestige.
The funnel does a different job in each market: in India, ignite and convert (awareness is nearly free); in the world, teach and recruit (top-of-funnel must carry education, not just awareness).

Comms guardrails. Brands and campaign associate with the film's grandeur, never with the deities' images. Ravana is framed with full dignity (scholar, Shiva-devotee), so Yash's casting reads as honouring the character's stature; the North-and-South pairing reads as unity, not hierarchy; colourism is never a visual theme. No alcohol, betting or vice partners.

05

International markets, tiered P1–P4

The original plan threw billboards at Times Square with no logic. This tiers the ₹105cr by where the revenue and the receptive audiences actually are.

TierMarketsSpendShareWhy
P1North America (US + Canada); GCC (UAE, Saudi, Qatar, Oman, Bahrain, Kuwait)₹58cr55%NA proved crossover with RRR + huge diaspora; GCC has the largest expat base and premium ticket prices.
P2UK & Ireland; Australia & New Zealand₹26cr25%Large, affluent, film-going diaspora; proven Indian-film markets.
P3Southeast Asia (Singapore, Malaysia, Indonesia, Thailand); Germany₹15cr14%The Ramayana is a local story across SEA (Ramakien, Kakawin Ramayana); Germany was RRR's crossover base.
P4Japan, South Africa, Mauritius, Fiji, rest of world₹6cr6%Opportunistic, PR and word-of-mouth led (RRR was a phenomenon in Japan).
International total₹105cr100%

Non-Indian crossover is won cheaply: trade and tastemaker screenings (as the real film did at CinemaCon, securing a Warner Bros global release), private screenings for directors and critics, film-enthusiast word of mouth on X/Reddit, and a Ranbir + Namit Malhotra US late-night appearance sold on the craft hooks (Zimmer, DNEG, "most expensive film made outside Hollywood") (DESIblitz). The Super Bowl was dropped: next game Feb 2027 (post-release) at ₹65–85cr a spot (Variety).

Funnel split by geography

Top-of-funnel carries education abroad, so the shape differs. Digital splits ~₹47cr India / ~₹33cr international.

MarketTop (awareness/educate)Mid (consider)Bottom (convert)Logic
India (₹47cr)~33%~40%~27%Awareness is near-free, so spend skews to consideration & booking.
International (₹33cr)~55%~30%~15%Top-of-funnel must teach the myth, not just announce the film.

Content pillars feeding both: spectacle, reverence/nostalgia, craft/prestige, lore/accessibility (a "Ramayana in three minutes" primer + long-form podcast for newcomers), and fandom participation. Two trailer cuts: India (emotion, scale, music) and crossover (myth, craft, IMAX, no song breaks). Comedy creators are used for hype on scale and craft, never on the deities. Bottom-funnel runs through BookMyShow and District, retargeting, search and IMAX advance-ticket pushes; measured on advance bookings, occupancy and geo-lift (box-office attribution is inherently fuzzy).

09

The earned multiplier (high PR-return plays)

Funded from the ₹60cr Activations + PR line. Each is bought for the press and conversation it generates, and each honours the guardrail.

  • Silent PR (Phase I): cast temple visits and quiet acts of respect that earn coverage before a rupee of paid runs.
  • Trade & tastemaker screenings: the CinemaCon-style route that won the real film a global distributor, plus private screenings for directors and critics.
  • Festival-timed reveals: teaser, posters and audio on Hanuman Jayanti, Ram Navami and Maha Shivratri, riding free organic conversation.
  • NMACC exhibition & live-orchestra tour: premium, press-friendly experiences signalling scale.
  • Monument activations: Times Square, Burj Khalifa, the London Eye, around Dussehra/Diwali. A ₹2–5cr buy that earns global headlines worth several times its cost.
  • Drone spectacle: a Ramayana-themed drone show at a grand neutral stage, a metro waterfront, the premiere, or a diaspora Diwali event, staged for its spectacle and shareability and kept clear of any place of worship.
  • Diaspora-as-distribution: temple networks, cultural associations and student bodies in P1/P2 as near-free grassroots reach.
